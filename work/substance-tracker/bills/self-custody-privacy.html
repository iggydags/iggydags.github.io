Self-custody surveillance limits

Holding your own keys shouldn't trigger AML reporting.

What it does

Establishes that running a personal Bitcoin wallet, holding one's own keys, or transacting with one's own wallet does not trigger Bank Secrecy Act / AML reporting obligations the way custodial financial relationships do.

In plain English

If you hold Bitcoin in a wallet you control, you wouldn't be subjected to bank-style surveillance reporting just for holding it. Same as cash in your wallet.

Why it matters

The current FinCEN posture treats self-hosted wallets as suspect by default. Without statutory clarification, every self-custody Bitcoin user is one rule change away from reporting obligations on their own coins.

End-user position

Foundational. Without it, self-custody is functionally one administrative rule away from being criminalized.
