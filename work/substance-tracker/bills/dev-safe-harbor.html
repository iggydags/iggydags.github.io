Substantive bill · Dev safe harbor Introduced

Open-source developer safe harbor

Writing open-source code shouldn't make you a money transmitter.

What it does

Establishes that publishing open-source Bitcoin/cryptocurrency code does not, by itself, constitute money transmission, brokerage, or other regulated financial activity. Protects software developers from being prosecuted for the actions of users of their code.

In plain English

If you write open-source Bitcoin software and publish it, you wouldn't be on the hook for what someone else does with your code. Same way a hammer manufacturer isn't liable for what someone hits with a hammer.

Why it matters

Without this, every Bitcoin developer is structurally exposed. The Tornado Cash and Samourai Wallet prosecutions chilled US-based open-source development. Developers have moved overseas or stopped publishing.

End-user position

Existential. The whole open-source Bitcoin development pipeline depends on this.
