About

A free civic reference for Bitcoin policy.

Operated by PubKey. Editorially independent of PubKey's investors and operators per the firewall in methodology.

Funding disclosure

This tracker is operated and funded by PubKey, a Bitcoin-native bar and media company with locations in NYC and Washington DC. It is funded by PubKey and its investors. The tracker operates with editorial independence per the firewall in /methodology.

Editorial independence

Scores are computed per the published rubric. PubKey does not approve scores before publication. Errata are public.

Why this exists

The crypto industry's first-term lobbying surge has cooled. The administration's attention has drifted to broader market-structure legislation and AI. The substantive Bitcoin items that matter to actual end users — de minimis tax exemption, miner tax clarity, open-source developer protections, self-custody privacy — are being carried by a smaller bench of members heading into contested 2026 races. This tracker names them.

What we track

  • Roll-call votes on the four substantive bills
  • Bill sponsorship and cosponsorship
  • Public statements from members on the four items
  • Industry PAC contributions to member campaigns (FEC filings)
  • Bench-score: composite of vote record, sponsorship, statement quality, and donation independence

What we do not track

  • Personal political views beyond the four substantive items
  • Crypto-adjacent legislation (market structure, stablecoin, broader exchange policy) — covered elsewhere
  • Token holdings of members (FEC discloses; not in our scoring scope)
  • Candidate fundraising not relevant to the four items
