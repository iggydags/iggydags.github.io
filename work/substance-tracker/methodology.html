Methodology

How substance is scored.

Four factors, weighted. Roll-call votes, sponsorship, public statements, donation independence. Recomputed weekly during the midterm cycle. Errata are public.

§ 01The rubric

Four factors.

FactorWeightScaleNotes
vote record35%0–10Roll-call votes on the four bills. Missing a vote scores 5 (not 0). Voting opposite end-user position scores 0.
sponsorship30%0–10Lead sponsor = 10. Cosponsor = 7. Public support without legislative action = 4. None = 0.
statements20%0–10Substance and consistency of public statements. On-record specifics about the four items score higher than generic crypto-positive remarks.
donation independence15%0–10Inverse of industry-PAC funding share. Member is rewarded for substance posture independent of industry money. Below-median industry funding = 10.
§ 02Score bands

What the numbers mean.

9-10Top bench · drives substance-side legislative action
7-8Substance-aligned · reliable on substance votes, mixed on leadership
5-6Mixed · supports some items, opposes or absents others
3-4Industry-aligned · public crypto support without substance backing
0-2Opposed · votes against substance items or carries industry-aligned alternative
§ 03Editorial advisory

The firewall.

Two outside voices review score-rubric application and bill-language interpretation before publication. Their names go here at launch. Until then: ⚐ TBD.

  • editorial · academic, ideally constitutional/tax-law · Reviews score-rubric application + statement attribution
  • editorial · practitioner, e.g. open-source dev or compliance attorney · Reviews bill-language interpretation
§ 04Errata

How errors are handled.

Errors are corrected publicly with a date-stamped errata entry on the affected page. No silent edits to scores. Methodology revisions trigger a recomputation announcement.

§ 05Out of scope

What we do not score.

  • Member personal Bitcoin holdings (FEC discloses; not in score)
  • Crypto-adjacent bills outside the four substantive items
  • Token endorsements / promotion
  • Non-policy political conduct

Cadence · Weekly during midterm cycle. Monthly in interim. Each recompute logged in /updates.
