Substantive bill · De minimis tax Introduced

De minimis tax exemption for small Bitcoin transactions

Stop taxing $5 coffee purchases.

What it does

Exempts small Bitcoin transactions from capital-gains reporting. The model is the existing $200 foreign-currency de minimis exemption — Bitcoin currently has none. Without this, every coffee purchase is a taxable event.

In plain English

If you buy coffee, lunch, or anything under a small dollar threshold using Bitcoin, you wouldn't have to report a separate capital gain or loss on your taxes. Same way you don't track tiny gains on foreign currency under $200.

Why it matters

Bitcoin can't function as a medium of exchange while every transaction triggers a separate tax-reporting obligation. This is the single biggest policy lever for everyday Bitcoin use.

End-user position

Top priority. Without this, Bitcoin functionally cannot be used as currency in the US.

Industry position

editorial · trade associations have deprioritized this in favor of broader market-structure legislation
