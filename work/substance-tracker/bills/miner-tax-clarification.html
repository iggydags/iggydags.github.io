Substantive bill · Miner tax clarity Introduced

Tax treatment for individual Bitcoin miners

Mining isn't free money the moment a block is found.

What it does

Clarifies that newly-mined Bitcoin is not taxable as income at the moment of mining (the current ambiguous IRS posture), but only when the miner actually realizes a sale. Aligns mining with other property creation.

In plain English

If you mine a Bitcoin in your basement, you wouldn't owe income tax on it the moment it's mined — only when you sell it. Same way an artist doesn't pay tax on a painting the moment they finish it.

Why it matters

Current tax treatment forces individual miners to liquidate immediately to cover taxes on coins they haven't sold. Effectively pushes mining to large industrial operations that can absorb the friction.

End-user position

Critical for individual / hobbyist miners. Without it, decentralized mining is structurally disadvantaged versus industrial operations.
